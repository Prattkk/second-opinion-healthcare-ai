# Second Opinion — Pitch Deck Summary

**COZAD New Venture Competition 2026 | Team 277**

---

## Thesis

Transform fragmented medical records into a clear, doctor-ready clinical timeline at the point of care.

## Problem

Medical records are fragmented across hospital portals, paper files, and pharmacy printouts. At every new appointment, doctors reconstruct patient history from scratch — wasting clinical time and creating risk of medication errors, missed allergies, and incomplete diagnoses.

A JMIR systematic review of 2,100+ studies over 25 years confirms that health data interoperability remains one of healthcare's most persistent failures.

## Why It Matters

- **Delays in care:** Physicians make faster decisions with incomplete information
- **Medication errors:** A drug prescribed at one clinic may interact with one from another
- **Patient anxiety:** Every new visit restarts the story from zero
- **Cognitive burden:** History reconstruction competes with diagnosis and treatment

## Solution

Patients upload whatever they have — PDFs, photos, scans. OCR + AI extract clinical data and organize it into a chronological timeline. Output: a clean, doctor-ready summary covering diagnoses, medications, allergies, and lab results.

## Target Customers

**Primary:** Chronic disease patients, multi-specialist patients, rare disease communities, caregivers
**Future:** Clinics, telehealth providers, care coordinators

## Early Validation

- Patients confirmed they dread new appointments due to having to re-explain everything
- Clinicians confirmed first minutes of visits are wasted on history reconstruction
- Discovery interviews conducted with physicians across primary care and specialty settings

## Closing Line

*"Every patient deserves to walk into any doctor's office and be fully understood — not in 10 minutes, but in 60 seconds."*
